## Meta & Process Skills

### skill-creator-pro

**Category:** Meta Skill (Discipline-Enforcing)

**What it does:** Teaches agents how to create production-quality skills using TDD methodology (RED-GREEN-REFACTOR).

**When to use:**
- Creating new skills from scratch
- Editing existing skills
- Verifying skills work before deployment
- Learning skill creation methodology

**Key features:**
- Complete TDD workflow for documentation
- Pressure scenario testing for discipline skills
- Progressive disclosure patterns
- Bulletproofing against rationalization
- 8-step creation process

**Dependencies:** None (foundational)

**Related skills:** None (this creates other skills)

**Complexity:** High (but worth learning)

**Estimated creation time with this skill:** 2-16 hours depending on skill type

**Best for:** Anyone creating skills, process documenters, knowledge codifiers

## Strategic Frameworks

### positioning

**Category:** Strategic Framework (Research & Analysis)

**What it does:** Guides product positioning using April Dunford's proven 10-step framework from "Obviously Awesome".

**When to use:**
- Positioning or repositioning a product
- Understanding market category
- Finding competitive differentiation
- Defining target market
- Clarifying value proposition

**Key features:**
- 10-step systematic process
- Initial assessment questionnaire
- Competitive alternative analysis
- Market category selection
- Value proposition structure
- Output template

**Dependencies:** Customer empathy (recommended before positioning)

**Related skills:** customer-empathy, copywriting (after positioning)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time to complete positioning:** 4-8 hours (vs weeks without structure)

**Best for:** Product leaders, founders, marketers, product marketers

### tradeoff-framework

**Category:** Decision Technique

**What it does:** Structures complex decisions with explicit criteria and scoring.

**When to use:**
- Product decisions (features, architecture, direction)
- Evaluating multiple options
- Framing tradeoffs
- Getting team alignment on decisions

**Key features:**
- Explicit criteria definition
- Scoring matrix
- Tradeoff visualization
- Decision documentation
- Team alignment process

**Dependencies:** None

**Related skills:** opportunity-solution-tree (for prioritization), product-discovery-inspired (for validation)

**Complexity:** Low

**Time to complete decision:** 1-2 hours

**Best for:** Product managers, technical leaders, founders making strategic choices

### customer-empathy

**Category:** Research & Strategy

**What it does:** Builds marketing strategy from deep customer understanding using Bernadette Jiwa's empathy-first methodology.

**When to use:**
- Developing value propositions
- Understanding customer context
- Building brand stories
- Strategic marketing foundations
- Before positioning or copywriting

**Key features:**
- 8 customer empathy questions
- Worldview mapping
- Struggling moment identification
- Belief system understanding
- Emotional context discovery

**Dependencies:** None (foundational research)

**Related skills:** positioning (use after), copywriting (use after), storytelling-science

**Complexity:** Low

**Time to complete:** 1-2 hours per customer segment

**Best for:** Marketers, product strategists, founders, brand builders

## Research & Discovery (JTBD)

### jtbd-research

**Category:** Research Framework

**What it does:** Core Jobs to be Done research and analysis methodology, including Forces of Progress framework.

**When to use:**
- Understanding why customers buy
- Identifying real competition
- Analyzing switching behavior
- Mapping forces of progress
- Understanding customer motivations

**Key features:**
- Forces of Progress framework (push/pull/anxiety/habits)
- Competition mapping from customer perspective
- Switch interview guidance
- Timeline analysis
- Causal data extraction

**Dependencies:** None (foundational JTBD)

**Related skills:** continuous-discovery-interview (execution), job-mapping (structure), outcome-discovery (outputs)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time to complete research cycle:** 4-8 weeks (12+ interviews)

**Best for:** Product researchers, UX researchers, strategists, product managers

### outcome-discovery

**Category:** Research Technique

**What it does:** Uncovers and structures desired outcome statements using Jobs-to-be-Done methodology.

**When to use:**
- Structuring customer needs
- Writing outcome statements
- JTBD research synthesis
- Finding measurable needs

**Key features:**
- Outcome statement structure: direction + metric + object + context
- Extraction from interview data
- Quality criteria
- Examples across domains
- Common mistakes guide

**Dependencies:** job-mapping (recommended), continuous-discovery-interview (source data)

**Related skills:** jtbd-research (framework), job-mapping (structure), outcome-segmentation (quantitative analysis)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time per outcome:** 5-10 minutes once learned

**Best for:** JTBD researchers, product managers, UX researchers

### job-mapping

**Category:** Research Framework

**What it does:** Breaks down customer jobs into 8-step Universal Job Map structure.

**When to use:**
- Structuring customer jobs
- Interview planning
- Discovering outcome opportunities
- Organizing research

**Key features:**
- 8-step universal structure (Define → Locate → Prepare → Confirm → Execute → Monitor → Modify → Conclude)
- Job map template
- Step-by-step guidance
- Examples across domains

**Dependencies:** None

**Related skills:** outcome-discovery (next step), continuous-discovery-interview (data collection), jtbd-research (framework)

**Complexity:** Low

**Time to map job:** 30-60 minutes

**Best for:** Product researchers, designers, product managers

### continuous-discovery-interview

**Category:** Interview Technique

**What it does:** Conducts story-based customer interviews to discover opportunities using Teresa Torres' methodology.

**When to use:**
- Conducting customer interviews
- Discovering opportunities (not solutions)
- Understanding customer behavior
- Avoiding leading questions

**Key features:**
- Story-based interviewing
- Natural follow-up questions
- Opportunity identification
- Interview structure and flow
- Common mistakes to avoid

**Dependencies:** None

**Related skills:** jtbd-research (framework), job-mapping (structure), opportunity-solution-tree (synthesis)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time per interview:** 30-60 minutes

**Best for:** Product teams, UX researchers, continuous discovery practitioners

### opportunity-solution-tree

**Category:** Framework & Tool

**What it does:** Creates and maintains an Opportunity Solution Tree to visualize paths toward desired outcomes.

**When to use:**
- Starting work on an outcome
- Mapping discovered opportunities
- Prioritizing what to build
- Resolving business/customer tension
- Guiding continuous discovery

**Key features:**
- OST structure (Outcome → Opportunities → Solutions → Experiments)
- Creation process
- Maintenance workflow
- Prioritization approach
- Visual organization

**Dependencies:** continuous-discovery-interview (opportunity source)

**Related skills:** continuous-discovery-interview (input), product-discovery-inspired (validation), tradeoff-framework (prioritization)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time to create OST:** 2-4 hours initial, 1 hour monthly maintenance

**Best for:** Product teams practicing continuous discovery, product managers, designers

## Product & Validation

### product-discovery-inspired

**Category:** Process Framework

**What it does:** Validates product ideas before building using INSPIRED methodology (4-risk validation).

**When to use:**
- Testing if customers will buy/use
- Validating usability
- Checking engineering feasibility
- Confirming business viability
- Before committing to build

**Key features:**
- 4-risk validation: Viability / Usability / Feasibility / Value
- Techniques per risk type
- Pass/fail criteria
- Validation planning
- Output format

**Dependencies:** opportunity-solution-tree (recommended source of ideas)

**Related skills:** opportunity-solution-tree (source), continuous-discovery-interview (research), tradeoff-framework (decisions)

**Complexity:** Medium

**Time per validation:** 1-2 weeks

**Best for:** Product teams, empowered teams, product managers

### pricing-survey-create

**Category:** Research Technique & Template

**What it does:** Creates pricing research surveys using Price Intelligently methodology (Patrick Campbell).

**When to use:**
- Researching pricing strategy
- Van Westendorp price sensitivity
- Value metric discovery
- Willingness to pay research

**Key features:**
- Survey 1: Qualification, segmentation, relative preference, price sensitivity
- Survey 2: Value metric discovery
- Van Westendorp questions
- Complete survey templates
- Question design guidance

**Dependencies:** None

**Related skills:** pricing-survey-analyze (next step), pricing-synthetic-panel (testing)

**Complexity:** High (but comprehensive)

**Time to create survey:** 4-8 hours

**Best for:** Founders, product leaders, pricing strategists
