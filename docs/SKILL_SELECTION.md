## The 11 Skills in This Pack

### Meta Skills (How to Build Skills)

**skill-creator-pro** - TDD methodology for creating skills
- **When to use**: Creating new skills, editing existing skills, or verifying skills work
- **Pattern type**: Discipline-enforcing
- **Key feature**: RED-GREEN-REFACTOR cycle for documentation
- **Best for**: Skill creators, process documenters

### Strategic Frameworks (Decision-Making & Positioning)

**positioning** - April Dunford's positioning framework
- **When to use**: Positioning/repositioning product, defining market category, competitive differentiation
- **Pattern type**: Research & analysis
- **Key feature**: 10-step systematic process
- **Best for**: Product leaders, founders, marketers

**tradeoff-framework** - Structured decision-making
- **When to use**: Making product decisions, evaluating options, framing tradeoffs
- **Pattern type**: Technique
- **Key feature**: Explicit criteria and scoring
- **Best for**: Product managers, technical leaders

**customer-empathy** - Bernadette Jiwa's empathy-first marketing
- **When to use**: Developing value propositions, understanding customer context, strategic foundations
- **Pattern type**: Research & strategy
- **Key feature**: 8 customer empathy questions
- **Best for**: Marketers, product strategists, founders

### Research & Discovery (JTBD Methodology)

**jtbd-research** - Core Jobs to be Done framework
- **When to use**: Understanding why customers buy, identifying real competition, analyzing switching behavior
- **Pattern type**: Research methodology
- **Key feature**: Forces of Progress framework
- **Best for**: Product researchers, UX researchers, strategists

**outcome-discovery** - Discovering desired outcome statements
- **When to use**: Uncovering measurable customer needs, structuring outcomes, JTBD research
- **Pattern type**: Technique
- **Key feature**: Outcome statement structure (direction + metric + object + context)
- **Best for**: JTBD researchers, product managers

**job-mapping** - Universal Job Map framework
- **When to use**: Breaking down jobs into steps, structuring interviews, discovering outcome opportunities
- **Pattern type**: Framework
- **Key feature**: 8-step universal structure
- **Best for**: Product researchers, designers

**continuous-discovery-interview** - Teresa Torres interview technique
- **When to use**: Conducting customer interviews, discovering opportunities, avoiding leading questions
- **Pattern type**: Technique
- **Key feature**: Story-based interviewing with natural follow-ups
- **Best for**: Product teams, UX researchers

**opportunity-solution-tree** - OST creation and maintenance
- **When to use**: Mapping paths to outcomes, resolving business/customer tension, guiding discovery
- **Pattern type**: Framework & tool
- **Key feature**: Visual opportunity mapping
- **Best for**: Product teams practicing continuous discovery

### Product & Validation

**product-discovery-inspired** - INSPIRED validation methodology
- **When to use**: Validating ideas before building, testing viability/usability/feasibility/value
- **Pattern type**: Process framework
- **Key feature**: Four-risk validation approach
- **Best for**: Product teams, empowered teams

**pricing-survey-create** - Patrick Campbell pricing research
- **When to use**: Creating pricing surveys, Van Westendorp analysis, value metric discovery
- **Pattern type**: Technique & template
- **Key feature**: Price Intelligently methodology
- **Best for**: Founders, product leaders, pricing strategists

### Execution & Communication

**copywriting** - Marketing copy creation framework
- **When to use**: Writing/improving copy for any page (homepage, landing, pricing, features)
- **Pattern type**: Technique & checklist
- **Key feature**: Voice of Customer integration
- **Best for**: Marketers, founders, product marketers

**storytelling-science** - Brain science narrative framework
- **When to use**: Creating founder stories, case studies, personal narratives, emotional resonance
- **Pattern type**: Framework
- **Key feature**: Cognitive science-backed structure
- **Best for**: Marketers, content creators, founders

## The Bottom Line

**Start with the skill that matches your immediate need.**

Don't try to learn all 11 at once. Pick one, use it on real work, see results. Then add another.

The skills compound: each one you learn makes the others more powerful. JTBD research informs better positioning. Customer empathy makes better copywriting. Outcome discovery feeds opportunity trees.

**Trust the frameworks.** These skills codify decades of proven methodology from April Dunford, Teresa Torres, Marty Cagan, Patrick Campbell, and others. Follow them even when your instinct says otherwiseâ€”especially when you're learning.

**Use skill-creator-pro to create your own.** The goal isn't to use these 11 skills forever. It's to learn the pattern and build your own library of company-specific, domain-specific skills.

That's how you create a competitive moat: not through better AI models, but through better process knowledge.
